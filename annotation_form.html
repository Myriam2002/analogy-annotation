Worked Examples

TARGET: Atom — The fundamental unit of matter: a dense nucleus (protons + neutrons) orbited by electrons in shells, held by electromagnetic forces.

"Solar System" → Coh 3 · Map 3 · Exp 3

Coh 3: Central mass with orbiting bodies — instantly mirrors nucleus-electron layout.
Map 3: Sun→nucleus; planets→electrons; gravity→electromagnetic force; orbital paths→shells.
Exp 3: Learner grasps spatial layout, governing forces, and relative scale.

"Marble in a Stadium" → Coh 2 · Map 2 · Exp 2

Coh 2: Conveys emptiness/scale but overall atomic structure isn't immediate.
Map 2: Marble→nucleus works for scale, but nothing represents electrons, shells, or forces.
Exp 2: Teaches one property (emptiness) well but misses the dynamic electron-nucleus relationship.

"Lego Brick" → Coh 1 · Map 1 · Exp 1

Coh 1: Solid uniform block — misrepresents internal structure entirely.
Map 1: No nucleus, electrons, or forces to map onto.
Exp 1: Could mislead learners into thinking atoms are solid. Zero insight.

Ranking: 1st Solar System · 2nd Marble in a Stadium · 3rd Lego Brick

TARGET: DNA — A double-helix molecule storing genetic info via four bases (A,T,C,G) with complementary pairing, able to unzip for replication.

"Twisted Zipper" → Coh 3 · Map 3 · Exp 3

Coh 3: Two interlocking sides wound together — immediately resonates with the double helix.
Map 3: Teeth→base pairs; fabric strips→backbone strands; zipping/unzipping→replication and transcription.
Exp 3: Learner visualizes strand separation and rejoining, building intuition about both structure and function.

"Spiral Staircase" → Coh 3 · Map 2 · Exp 2

Coh 3: Spiral shape maps onto the helix — clicks at first glance.
Map 2: Steps→base pairs is okay, but one railing ≠ two distinct strands. No analog for base pairing or separation.
Exp 2: Captures shape well but doesn't convey encoding or replication. Partial learning.

"Recipe Book" → Coh 2 · Map 1 · Exp 1

Coh 2: "Stores instructions" connects loosely — both contain info to produce something.
Map 1: Flat, sequential book has no structural parallel for double helix, base pairing, or strand separation.
Exp 1: "DNA is instructions" is too vague to build real understanding.

Ranking: 1st Twisted Zipper · 2nd Spiral Staircase · 3rd Recipe Book

TARGET: Electricity — Flow of electric charge (electrons) through a conductor, driven by voltage and resisted by material properties (Ohm's law: V=IR).

"Water in Pipes" → Coh 3 · Map 3 · Exp 3

Coh 3: One of science's most natural analogies — "flow" maps seamlessly.
Map 3: Water→electrons; pipe diameter→resistance; pressure→voltage; flow rate→current.
Exp 3: Narrow the pipe = more resistance, increase pressure = more current. Powerful predictive tool.

"Highway Traffic" → Coh 2 · Map 2 · Exp 2

Coh 2: Cars moving captures flow, but autonomous agents choosing paths ≠ field-driven electrons.
Map 2: Cars→electrons and lanes→wire works, but no voltage counterpart. Jams ≠ resistance.
Exp 2: Basic "things in a channel" sense but could mislead on mechanism.

"Lightning Bolt" → Coh 2 · Map 1 · Exp 1

Coh 2: Lightning is electricity — related but it's a dramatic instance, not a separate familiar concept.
Map 1: Sudden chaotic discharge has no mapping to steady current, resistance, or voltage in a circuit.
Exp 1: Reinforces "electricity is scary" rather than circuit understanding. Misleading.

Ranking: 1st Water in Pipes · 2nd Highway Traffic · 3rd Lightning Bolt
